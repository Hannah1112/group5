Design

The Samsung Galaxy S23 finds itself in a tough spot. As the latest version of a well-established flagship phone — the first Galaxy S model came out 13 years ago — there's only so many changes Samsung can make to its device to make this year's edition stand out from the last.

It's cleaner and draws less attention to the cameras.

You'll get the usual assortment of high-end materials with the Galaxy S23 lineup, and Samsung says it's using Corning's Gorilla Glass Victus 2 glass in the body of the S23. That should make the phone more durable and less prone to picking up scratches, scuffs and cracks.

Software and performance

The software this generation has changed even less than the hardware. The One UI 5.1 offers few upgrades over One UI 5.0 besides a streamlined setup experience and some refinements to the gallery and camera app. Though, truthfully, it's not like Samsung had much it needed to add. One UI is so feature-filled at this point that it almost feels bloated when you try to go digging for a setting or function that you don't already know the location of.

Samsung did away with Exynos for basically all Galaxy S23 models worldwide, opting for the Qualcomm Snapdragon 8 Gen 2 Mobile Platform for Galaxy. A chipset can make or break your smartphone experience, as we saw last year with the battery and thermal issues surrounding the Snapdragon 8 Gen 1.
